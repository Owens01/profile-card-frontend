About Me

Bio

I am a passionate and curious learner with a growing interest in web development and technology as a whole. Over time, I’ve come to appreciate how creativity and logic combine to build meaningful digital experiences. What excites me most is the process of transforming ideas into functional, visually appealing, and accessible applications that can make a real difference in people’s lives. I enjoy solving problems, experimenting with new tools, and continuously improving my skills through consistent practice. Outside of coding, I’m someone who values teamwork, self-discipline, and lifelong learning qualities I believe are essential in both personal and professional growth. My journey so far has been filled with small wins, valuable lessons, and the realization that progress in tech is not about perfection, but persistence.

Goals in this program

  • Improve my HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills.
  • Learn to build user-friendly applications.
  • Collaborate effectively on projects.

Areas of low confidence

One area I’m still working on is managing the feeling of uncertainty when I encounter complex problems or new technologies. Sometimes, I find myself doubting my abilities when I can’t find solutions quickly or when a concept takes longer to grasp. I also tend to overthink the details, which can slow me down instead of helping me move forward. Another area I want to strengthen is my confidence in presenting or explaining technical ideas to others. I occasionally worry about not using the right terms or being misunderstood. However, I’ve started to see these as growth opportunities rather than weaknesses. With continuous practice, collaboration, and mentorship, I’m learning to face these challenges with patience and confidence, trusting that consistent effort will eventually turn these weak spots into strengths.

Note to future self

Keep practicing, keep building, stay curious, and ask for help when needed.

Extra thoughts

This journey has reminded me that learning to code is more than just memorizing syntax, it’s about developing patience, problem-solving skills, and creative thinking. Each challenge, from debugging errors to understanding new concepts, has helped me build resilience and confidence. Sometimes progress feels slow, but looking back, every small step has added up to visible growth. I’ve also realized that collaboration and asking questions are not signs of weakness but tools for improvement. Moving forward, I want to stay consistent, practice daily, and keep pushing beyond my comfort zone because that’s where true growth happens.
